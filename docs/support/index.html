Common questions

How do I add a QR code to my design?

Open the QR Code Generator from the apps panel in Canva. Type or paste a URL, pick your foreground and background colors (or enable the transparent-background switch), then click Add QR code. The code will be inserted at the top-left of your page — drag it to wherever you'd like and resize from the corner handles.

Why is my QR code transparent by default?

The default settings produce a transparent QR code with no quiet zone — convenient for placing on top of your own backgrounds. If you need maximum scan reliability, turn off "Transparent background" (so the white margin is solid) and turn on "Quiet zone" (which adds a 4-module margin around the code).

The QR code I generated won't scan. What's wrong?

  • Add a quiet zone. Most scanners require a margin of at least four modules of empty space around the code.
  • Use enough contrast. Dark foreground on a light background scans best. Very light foreground colors or busy backgrounds behind a transparent QR can defeat the scanner.
  • Don't shrink it too small. Print smaller than ~2 cm and many phone cameras can't lock onto the modules.
  • Test before publishing. Always scan with a real phone camera before exporting your design.

Can I resize the QR code without losing quality?

Yes. The QR is inserted as a vector SVG, so it stays crisp at any size you scale it to in Canva.

Can I edit the colors of an existing QR code on my canvas?

Not directly — the colors are baked into the inserted vector. To change the colors, re-open the app, pick new colors, and click Add QR code again, then delete the old one.
